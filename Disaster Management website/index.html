Rescue Information

Earthquake Rescue Information

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On during shaking.
  • Stay indoors if you are inside a building, away from windows, heavy furniture, and objects that may fall.
  • If you are outside, move to an open area away from buildings, streetlights, and utility wires.
  • After the shaking stops, be cautious of potential aftershocks and be prepared to take cover again.

Flood Rescue Information

  • Move to higher ground immediately if you are in a flood-prone area.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters; they may be deeper or faster-moving than they appear.
  • Turn off utilities if instructed to do so to prevent electrical hazards.
  • Keep an emergency kit with essential supplies, including a flashlight, non-perishable food, and water.

Cyclone Rescue Information

  • Monitor weather updates and warnings provided by meteorological authorities.
  • Secure outdoor objects and bring them indoors to prevent them from becoming projectiles in strong winds.
  • Board up windows and reinforce doors to protect against strong winds and flying debris.
  • Stay indoors during the cyclone, away from windows and doors, until authorities announce it is safe to go outside.

Tsunami Rescue Information

  • Immediately move to higher ground if you are in a coastal area and receive a tsunami warning.
  • Avoid the coastline and low-lying areas; tsunamis can inundate the shore with powerful and fast-moving water.
  • Do not attempt to watch or film the tsunami; prioritize your safety and move inland as quickly as possible.
  • Follow evacuation routes and signs to reach elevated areas or designated safe zones.
